Devren

Deep in the forest, far removed from civilization lay a hamlet composed of humans and elves. This agrarian community survived off the land and lived simple but complete lives under the stars, amongst nature. But it was not long before their successful and undefended trove became the knowledge of a local mercenary family. A family of the Red Scale which would introduce themselves as friendly, bodyguards who would ensure no wrong would come to the inhabitants of the town...for a price. A price that became increasingly more expensive as the years ran forward. For a time, the hamlet remained prosperous and bolstered by the knowledge they had mighty "allies" who could be summoned in a time of need. A for a time, this was true, the Red Scale stopped Feral Orcs and gnolls from enchroaching, and even returned a kidnapped family to the town when a rival guild attempted to lay claim.

Then the tithes requirement increased and continued to increase year over year until they could simply not be met....and then the true price of this "alliance" was made clear. Lives. Either in tithe or in punishment. The hamlet considered resisting, but among them they had no fighters. They considered sending for help, but the distance would require much travel and the possibility of being intercepted was great...it was made clear such actions would result in further escalation of the tithe. So they obeyed. Giving the Red Scale one young woman per year.

He would never meet her, but the monks would tell him her name was "Emilia." She had been tithed to the family of scale and rapidly impregnated by one of the elders of the clan. She refused to give her child to these people, this organization of deceit and despair. She fled under cover of darkness and traveled as far as she could before encountering a long vestige of civilization in a remote monastery populated by a dozen Monks who had taken to the seclusion and peace this place offered. She left her newborn in their care and continued to flee. She took the basket she had been carrying him in and filled it with clothes to maintain the ruse of his continued presence. She begged the monks to give him a life that would not carry him into the darkest callings of his blood and they agreed solemnly to impart their wisdom unto him.

Perhaps she was successful and escaped unscathed. Perhaps she was found. Devren would never be truly certain what outcome befell her.

History would unfold peacefully as Devren grew up amongst his new family. As fortune would have it, one of the monks was quite skilled in Draconic and ensured he could speak his native language, albey with a bizarre accent. His skill and strength would developed and be slowly harnessed by his teachings. He would learn that peace is the first and best path and that all life has value, even in its ending. He would learn the history of his Draconic heritage, the stories that were told of ancient beasts of fire and scale flying through the air, alerting every soul for miles with a roar that shook the very earth. He was always taken with the parts about flying and insisted if he just put his mind to it he would be able to take flight. As a child he would often be found jumping from tall structures, certain this time he would stay aloft...despite not having wings. His peers would find this amusing and gave him the nickname of "Stone" in that he sank like one. He would take this as his Surname in the years that would come.

For 20 years life was, by all measures, blessed. He had become adept at the culture he was born into and had found respect and comradery among the Monks he believed he would spend the rest of his life with. His mentor, Anquis, approached him one day, telling him he had heard rumor of his mothers location and gave him what little money the monastery possessed in the hopes he might undertake the journey that had always lingered in his heart. To find his origin, to know the truth. Devren accepted this mission and assured his mentor he would return and that he would take his teachings with him every step of every day.

This was a deception. The Red Scale had found him and the monastery and had approaced them in secret demanding they turn him over to them or suffer their wrath. The Monk agreed and then set in motion Devrens escape without his knowledge of the seriousness of the situation. He knew Devren would stay and fight for his family...against his family...and this would only end in great suffering. He also knew that the Red Scale would not accept Devren had simply wandered off and that a terrible toll would be exacted.

Devren stands nearly 7 feet tall and has mostly bronze/rust colored scales across his body, save around his eyes which retain a powerful crimson shade and gives him a somewhat nefarious facial visage when he blinks. He is usually found with a smile on his face which inadvertently reveals several sharp and well maintained teeth. His truthfully happy expression can appear quite aggressive. He wields a quarterstaff that he has carved in the same motif as the scales on his body, with more earthy toned colors and his primary article of clothing is a two piece cloak that hangs where wings might once have been attached to his distant ancestors. He favors royal colors, of gold and silver if possible to offset the implied nature of his reddish tones.

Gabriel Atherion Sentril Algeroth

Gabriel Atherion Sentril Algeroth of house Algeroth is the son of the noble lord Samot Yntrix Gorot Algeroth and Lady Ettenan Ryna Algeroth. Gabriel had a very privileged upbringing, living in the family's manor together with servants and tutors, showing great interest in literature, art and science. Although he is handy with a blade, and a quick learner of both battle and warfare, he has never been interested in war or adventure. Instead, he was always complacent with the life that was planned for him; getting a good education and taking over the family import and export business the same way his father had done before him and his father before that. The family name holds great weight in higher circles of society and is well respected for being able to deliver goods in a timely manner. The family follows a code of honor and although none of the family patriarchs have ever shied away from a good deal, they tend to stay within the confines of law and order.

Of course, due to the many years of successful and respectable trade, there have been a few instances where shadier elements have tried to use the business for their own needs. Gabriel can recall several instances when strange people have even shown up at the manor and had private meetings with his father, most often resulting in these same people storming out with grim expressions after being denied help. These instances were not a common occurrence but they became a part of daily life.

Because of the visits of strange people, Gabriel, now in his late teens, didn't even shrug when one day there was a knock on the door and outside stood a woman in a long black dress, a large black hat with a raven feather in it and a veiled face, who asked to speak to Gabriels father. The woman introduced herself as Lady Morina de Mon and was accompanied by a very strange entourage of three people. The first was a tall and lanky man dressed in black pants and a tight shirt. His skin was pale, almost white and his eyes were bright blue, almost as if they were glowing. The second two were two girls, both with frazzled black hair, wearing attire similar to Lady Morina but less fancy. The girls were continuously grinning and whispering and similarly to the tall lanky man they had bright blue eyes. Gabriel got a strange feeling from them but shrugged it off, knowing that they would soon leave the same as all other strange people before them. A few hours later, Gabriel could see the group heading off with their carriage as expected. However, during supper there was something different about his father Samot. He seemed disheartened somehow but when Gabriel inquired if had to do with the strange visitors, Samot said that he had dealt with them and that they would not be seeing them again. At first Gabriel was not convinced but when Samot gave Gabriel his normal friendly fatherly smile and changed the topic to something more joyful all of Gabriels worries were washed away.

That night there a freakish thunderstorm blew up, tearing at the manor to the extent that the windows were rattling. Gabriel was a heavy sleeper but something stirred his slumber and he awoke in the middle of the night. At first he thought he was just seeing shadows, but then he saw two intense blue orbs in the darkness. He stared at them with a sense of recognition, but just as he realised what they were, and before he could scream, Lady Morina was upon him, holding him down, covering his mouth and speaking an incantation of some sort.

This is where the story of Gabriel Atherion Sentril Algeroth ends, and the story of Blank the ragged begins. Confused and scared, Blank ran through the city. He didn't recognise where he was, when it was or even who he was! Stumbling past a shop, he caught his face in a reflection. Studying it he saw a man in his early twenties, dressed in rags and with the beginning of a scruffy beard. He didn't remember, but somehow he felt that the face looking back at him was different, older, then it should be. Looking down on his person, he could see he was wearing old and tattered clothes and had a bag at his side made from leather. Looking quickly in the bag, the first thing he found was a burnt and stained piece of paper, weathered by age to the point that it was barely readable. Readable, Blank realised he could read! Quickly skimming through the paper the only thing he could discern from the ruined document was the three first letters of a name at the bottom, "San". Digging deeper in the bag he also found a bag of 25 gold coins. Realising this, Blank quickly put it back in the leather bag. The last item in the bag was an old and blackened ring made from silver. The ring wasn't remarkable at all but had a distinct and well carved "A" on it. Studying the ring, Blank was suddenly struck by a horrible headache and in his minds eye two blue orbs flashed for a moment before he was once more standing on the street and staring at his reflection. During the memory flash, Blank had stumbled into the glass, and the shop owner had rushed out and started yelling at Blank who set off running through the city. He rounded a corner, and since he was looking behind him, ran straight into (someone from the party).
